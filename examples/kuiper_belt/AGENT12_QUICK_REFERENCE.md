## The Stabilizing Mechanism

### Primary: Mean-Motion Resonance (Most Likely)

When outer planet is in 2:1 or 3:2 resonance with object:
- Creates dynamical "walls" preventing chaos
- Stabilizes regions that would otherwise be chaotic
- Most efficient method for planetary system stabilization

**Evidence**: Objects cluster near predicted resonance locations

### Secondary: Secular Resonance

Long-period coupling of orbital elements (apsidal precession):
- Suppresses eccentricity growth
- Especially effective for high-inclination objects
- Creates stable orbital "channels"

### Tertiary: Dynamical Shepherding

Massive planet periodically nudges objects:
- Brief but strong gravitational encounters
- Reset chaotic growth before it compounds
- Like a celestial shepherd guiding a flock

### Special Case: Kozai-Lidov Coupling

For retrograde/high-i objects (65407, 127546):
- Inclination and eccentricity oscillate together
- Can modulate stability periodically
- Explains why extremely high-e objects can survive
